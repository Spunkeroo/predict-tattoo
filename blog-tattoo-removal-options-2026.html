How Laser Removal Works

Laser tattoo removal uses concentrated light pulses to break ink particles into tiny fragments. Your immune system then flushes these fragments through the lymphatic system over weeks. The laser targets specific ink colors based on wavelength — different colors require different laser wavelengths.

Modern lasers (picosecond technology) pulse in trillionths of a second, shattering ink more effectively than older nanosecond lasers. This means fewer sessions, less pain, and better results than even 5 years ago.

What to Expect

During the Session

The laser feels like rubber bands snapping rapidly against your skin. Most clinics apply topical numbing cream 30-45 minutes before treatment. Sessions for small tattoos take 5-15 minutes. Larger pieces may take 30-60 minutes. The area will feel hot and swollen immediately after.

After the Session

Expect redness, swelling, and blistering for 1-2 weeks. The treated area will look worse before it looks better. Blisters are normal and should not be popped. Apply prescribed ointment and keep the area bandaged for 3-5 days. Avoid sun exposure for 4-6 weeks after each session.

Number of Sessions

Factors that affect how many sessions you need:

  • Ink color: Black is easiest to remove (4-6 sessions). Green and blue are hardest (8-15 sessions). Yellow and white are nearly impossible with current technology
  • Ink quality: Professional tattoos use denser, deeper ink requiring more sessions. Amateur tattoos (stick-and-poke) often remove faster
  • Skin tone: Lighter skin allows higher laser intensity. Darker skin requires more conservative settings and more sessions to avoid hypopigmentation
  • Location: Tattoos closer to the heart remove faster due to better blood circulation. Extremities (hands, feet) take longer
  • Age of tattoo: Older tattoos have already been partially broken down by your immune system and remove faster than fresh ones

Aftercare

  1. Keep it clean: Gently wash with mild soap and lukewarm water 2-3 times daily
  2. Apply healing ointment: Aquaphor or prescribed antibiotic ointment for the first week
  3. Do not pop blisters: They protect the healing skin underneath. Let them drain naturally
  4. Avoid sun: UV exposure can cause hyperpigmentation on treated skin. Cover the area or use SPF 50+
  5. Wait 6-8 weeks between sessions: Your body needs time to flush broken ink particles. Rushing sessions does not improve results
  6. Stay hydrated and healthy: A strong immune system clears ink faster. Exercise, hydration, and good nutrition support the removal process

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does tattoo removal take?

Most tattoos require 6-12 sessions spaced 6-8 weeks apart. Complete removal of a medium tattoo takes approximately 12-18 months from start to finish.

Is tattoo removal painful?

Most people rate it as more painful than getting the tattoo itself. However, topical numbing cream significantly reduces discomfort. Sessions are also much shorter than tattoo sessions.

Can tattoos be completely removed?

Black and dark ink tattoos can be removed to 95%+ in most cases. Some residual ghosting may remain. Colored inks, especially green, blue, yellow, and white, are more difficult and may not fully disappear.

Does tattoo removal leave scars?

Modern picosecond lasers have very low scarring risk when performed by qualified professionals. Older Q-switched lasers carry slightly higher risk. Following aftercare instructions is essential for scar-free healing.

Is it cheaper to cover up or remove a tattoo?

Cover-up tattoos ($200-$1,000) are significantly cheaper than full removal ($1,500-$7,000+). Many people do 2-3 lightening sessions to fade the old tattoo, then get a cover-up. This combination approach is cost-effective.
