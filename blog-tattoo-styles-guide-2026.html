Complete Guide to Tattoo Styles 2026: Find Your Look

Choosing a tattoo style is as important as choosing the design itself. Each style has its own aesthetic, aging characteristics, and ideal placement. Chicago is home to world-class artists in every style — here's your guide to finding the right look and the right artist.

Traditional (American Traditional)

Bold black outlines, limited color palette (red, green, yellow, blue, black), iconic imagery (roses, eagles, anchors, skulls). Traditional tattoos age the best of any style — those thick outlines keep designs readable for 40+ years. Originated in the 1900s with Sailor Jerry and has never gone out of style. Chicago artists: Nick Colella (Great Lakes Tattoo), any artist at Chicago Tattoo Company.

Japanese (Irezumi)

Full-sleeve and full-back designs with koi fish, dragons, chrysanthemums, and waves. Follows strict compositional rules — wind bars, background fill, and subject hierarchy. Japanese tattoos are designed to flow with the body's natural contours. Takes 30-100+ hours for a full sleeve. Chicago artists: Mike Rubendall (guest spots at Great Lakes), Horizaru (appointments only).

Realism/Photorealism

Tattoos that look like photographs — portraits, animals, landscapes, objects. Requires exceptional artist skill. Black-and-grey realism uses subtle shading. Color realism adds dimension but is harder to execute. Realistic tattoos can fade faster than traditional if not protected from UV. Always use SPF 50 on healed realistic tattoos. Chicago artists: David Allen (guest spots), Carlos Rojas (multiple studios).

Fine Line

Delicate, thin lines without heavy shading or color fill. Minimalist aesthetic popular with first-time tattoo clients. Includes script, botanical illustrations, geometric shapes, and small symbolic designs. Fine line tattoos may need touch-ups sooner than traditional styles. Chicago artists: Playground Tattoo (West Loop), Rachel (Speakeasy Custom).
